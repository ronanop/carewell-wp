# Care Well Medical Centre — Brand Bible

**Document Type:** Permanent Creative Reference  
**Audience:** Designers, developers, copywriters, AI agents generating UI  
**Design Direction:** Editorial Luxury Minimalism · Enterprise Healthcare · Premium Trust  
**Cross-reference:** `docs/07_DESIGN_SYSTEM.md`, `docs/08_UI_GUIDELINES.md`, `skills/healthcare-ui.md`

---

## Mission

To present Care Well Medical Centre as the region's most trusted destination for advanced medical and aesthetic care — where clinical excellence meets editorial refinement, and every digital touchpoint reassures patients before they ever walk through the door.

## Vision

A digital presence that feels less like a medical template and more like a premium lifestyle publication about health — authoritative without being cold, luxurious without being exclusionary, modern without being trendy.

---

## Brand Personality

| Trait | Expression | Not This |
|-------|------------|----------|
| **Authoritative** | Credentials, certifications, years of practice front and centre | Arrogant, lecturing |
| **Refined** | Generous whitespace, restrained palette, editorial typography | Ornate, decorative |
| **Warm** | Human photography, empathetic copy, accessible language | Clinical, sterile |
| **Confident** | Clear CTAs, direct statements, no hedging | Aggressive, salesy |
| **Modern** | Clean grids, contemporary layouts, fast performance | Template-based, generic |
| **Trustworthy** | Transparent contact info, real doctors, verifiable claims | Hype, exaggeration |

**Brand archetype:** The Trusted Expert — part specialist physician, part premium concierge.

---

## Brand Values

1. **Clinical Integrity** — Every claim is accurate. No exaggerated outcomes. No before/after manipulation.
2. **Patient Dignity** — Copy and imagery respect patient privacy and autonomy.
3. **Transparency** — Contact information, credentials, and pricing guidance (where applicable) are easy to find.
4. **Excellence** — Visual and verbal quality reflects the standard of care delivered in-clinic.
5. **Accessibility** — Premium does not mean exclusive to those who can parse medical jargon or navigate complex interfaces.

---

## Tone of Voice

### Voice Attributes

- **Professional** — grammatically precise, medically accurate, never slang
- **Reassuring** — acknowledge patient concerns without amplifying fear
- **Clear** — short sentences, plain language for procedures and instructions
- **Warm** — second person ("you") where appropriate; human, not institutional
- **Confident** — active voice; definitive where evidence supports it

### Tone Spectrum by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Homepage hero | Confident, welcoming | "Advanced care, thoughtfully delivered." |
| Service description | Informative, reassuring | "This treatment is designed to address [condition] with minimal downtime." |
| Blog article | Educational, accessible | "Here's what you should know before your first consultation." |
| Error message | Calm, helpful | "We couldn't load this page. Please try again, or call us directly." |
| Form success | Warm, confirming | "Thank you. Our team will respond within one business day." |
| Legal/footer | Formal, precise | Standard compliance language |

### Words We Use

- Care, treatment, consultation, specialist, expertise, personalised, advanced, trusted, experienced, comprehensive, gentle, precise, results

### Words We Avoid

- Miracle, guaranteed, cheapest, best in the world, pain-free (unless clinically accurate), anti-aging (prefer "rejuvenation" or specific outcomes), fix, cure (unless medically appropriate), hack, deal, limited time

## Writing Style

- **Sentence length:** Average 12–18 words in body copy. Hero headlines may be shorter.
- **Paragraph length:** 2–4 sentences maximum on web. Break long WordPress content with subheadings.
- **Headings:** Sentence case for section headings ("Our dermatology services"), title case for page titles in navigation.
- **Numbers:** Use numerals for statistics ("15+ years", "500+ treatments"). Spell out one through nine only in flowing prose.
- **Medical terms:** Define on first use if not commonly understood. Link to service pages for depth.
- **Punctuation:** No exclamation marks in body copy. One exclamation maximum in marketing CTAs if ever used (prefer none).

## Target Audience

### Primary Audience

- **Age:** 28–55
- **Profile:** Urban and suburban professionals seeking dermatology, cosmetology, dental, or aesthetic treatments
- **Motivation:** Quality of care, doctor credentials, clinic reputation, visible results, convenience

### Secondary Audience

- Referring physicians and medical professionals evaluating partnership credibility
- Family members researching care options for relatives
- Returning patients seeking specific services or doctors

## Visual Personality

- **Editorial** — layouts feel magazine-quality: strong typography, intentional asymmetry, generous margins
- **Luxurious** — space is a material; elements breathe; nothing feels cramped
- **Restrained** — one accent colour (gold) used sparingly; no visual noise
- **Photographic** — real clinic, real doctors, real environment; never stock "doctor with stethoscope"
- **Structural** — grids are precise; alignment is exact; borders are hairline, not heavy

## Brand Emotions

Every page should leave the visitor feeling:

1. **Reassured** — "This clinic knows what they're doing"
2. **Respected** — "They treat patients with dignity"
3. **Confident** — "I can trust this team with my care"
4. **Calm** — "I'm not being rushed or pressured"
5. **Informed** — "I understand my options"

Never: anxious, overwhelmed, sceptical, patronised, or sold to.

## Trust Signals

### Must Be Visible (Above Fold or Header)

- Phone number (clickable on mobile)
- Years of establishment or practice
- Key certifications or affiliations
- Real doctor names and credentials

### Must Exist Somewhere on Site

- Physical clinic address with map
- Professional photography of facility and team
- Patient testimonials (when available from WordPress)
- Service-specific expertise indicators
- Blog content demonstrating thought leadership

### Trust Signal Hierarchy

1. Doctor credentials and photos
2. Clinic photography and environment
3. Certifications and affiliations
4. Patient testimonials
5. Volume/experience statistics ("15+ years", "thousands of patients")
6. Media mentions or awards (if applicable)

## Healthcare Communication Guidelines

- **Never diagnose** on the website. Use "may help with", "commonly treats", "designed for".
- **Never promise outcomes.** Use "results vary", "individual assessment required".
- **Always recommend consultation** for treatment decisions. Website informs; doctors decide.
- **Include disclaimers** on treatment pages where WordPress content references results.
- **Respect patient privacy** — no identifiable patient imagery without documented consent.
- **Emergency disclaimer** — website is not for medical emergencies; display emergency number guidance in footer if appropriate for jurisdiction.
- **Regulatory compliance** — follow local healthcare advertising regulations. When in doubt, understate.

## CTA Writing Style

### Primary CTAs (One Per Viewport)

| Context | Label | Avoid |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Homepage hero | "Book a consultation" | "Book now!", "Get started" |
| Service page | "Book this treatment" | "Buy now", "Claim offer" |
| Doctor page | "Book with Dr. [Name]" | "Schedule ASAP" |
| Blog article | "Speak to a specialist" | "Don't wait!" |
| Contact page | "Send your enquiry" | "Submit", "Go" |
| Gallery | "Visit our clinic" | "See more!!!" |

### Secondary CTAs

- "View all services"
- "Read the article"
- "Meet our doctors"
- "Explore treatments"
- "Call [phone number]"

### CTA Rules

- Verb-first, 2–4 words for buttons
- No ALL CAPS buttons
- No urgency language ("Limited spots", "Today only")
- Phone number always available as alternative to form

## Microcopy Guidelines

### Navigation

- Use plain names: "Services", "Doctors", "Blog", "About", "Contact", "Gallery"
- No cute labels: not "Our Journey", "The Team", "Insights" (unless WordPress menu uses them — match CMS)

### Form Labels

- "Full name" not "Name*"
- "Email address" not "Email"
- "Phone number (optional)" — always mark optional fields
- "Your message" not "Comments"
- "Subject" — specific, not "Topic"

### Helper Text

- Short, below field: "We'll respond within one business day."
- Phone field: "Include country code if outside India."

### Empty States

- "No services found in this category."
- "No blog posts yet. Check back soon."

---

## Error Message Tone

- **Calm** — never alarmist
- **Helpful** — offer an alternative action
- **Brief** — one sentence + action

| Scenario | Copy |
|----------|------|
| Page load failure | "We're having trouble loading this content. Please try again." |
| 404 | "This page doesn't exist. You may have followed an outdated link." |
| Form validation | "[Field] is required." / "Please enter a valid email address." |
| Form submission failure | "Your message couldn't be sent. Please try again or call us at [phone]." |
| Network error | "Please check your connection and try again." |

Never: "Error 500", "Oops!", "Something went wrong!!!", technical jargon.

---

## Success Message Tone

- **Warm** — acknowledge the person's action
- **Specific** — set expectations for next step
- **Brief**

| Scenario | Copy |
|----------|------|
| Contact form submitted | "Thank you. Our team will respond within one business day." |
| Newsletter (future) | "You're subscribed. We'll send our latest articles to your inbox." |

---

## Form Writing Style

- Labels above fields, never placeholder-only
- Required fields marked with asterisk and `aria-required`
- Optional fields explicitly labelled "(optional)"
- Submit button describes action: "Send enquiry" not "Submit"
- Privacy note below form: "Your information is used only to respond to your enquiry. We do not share your data with third parties."

## Content Writing Principles

1. **Lead with benefit, follow with method** — what the patient gains, then how the treatment works.
2. **One idea per paragraph** — especially in service descriptions rendered from WordPress.
3. **Use subheadings every 200–300 words** in long blog content.
4. **Write for scanning** — bullets for lists of benefits, symptoms, or steps.
5. **Maintain editorial voice** even in CMS-sourced content — propose WordPress edits if tone drifts.
6. **Preserve WordPress content faithfully** in rendering — do not rewrite clinical copy in code.

## Photography Style

### Subject Matter

- Real clinic interiors: reception, treatment rooms (where appropriate), equipment
- Real doctors: professional portraits, candid in-clinic moments
- Real patients: only with consent; diverse representation
- Treatment context: hands, equipment, environment — not graphic clinical imagery

### Technical Qualities

- Natural or soft studio lighting — never harsh flash
- Warm-neutral colour grading — align with brand palette
- Shallow depth of field acceptable for portraits
- High resolution: minimum 1920px wide for hero images
- Consistent aspect ratios within component types (see `page-blueprints.md`)

### Avoid

- Stock photography of generic doctors
- Before/after unless sourced from WordPress with proper consent and disclaimers
- Graphic medical procedure imagery on listing pages
- Over-retouched skin or unrealistic results
- Clip art, icons substituting for photography

---

## Icon Style

- **Library:** Lucide React — consistent stroke width
- **Stroke width:** 1.5px default (Lucide standard)
- **Size:** 16px inline, 20px navigation, 24px feature blocks, 32px empty states
- **Colour:** `text-muted-foreground` default; `text-primary` for active/accent
- **Usage:** Functional only — wayfinding, actions, feature illustration
- **Never:** decorative icon clusters, emoji, filled cartoon icons, medical cross clichés

---

## Illustration Style

- **Primary approach:** Photography over illustration
- **When illustration is needed:** Minimal line art, monochromatic, teal or gold accent
- **Never:** Cartoon characters, 3D renders, anatomical diagrams on marketing pages (clinical diagrams belong in blog content from WordPress)
- **Decorative elements:** Geometric line accents acceptable at section breaks — hairline weight, gold or teal at 20% opacity

---

## Image Direction

### Hero Images

- Subject: clinic environment, doctor portrait, or treatment context
- Overlay: subtle gradient from transparent to `background` if text overlays image
- Text contrast: ensure 4.5:1 minimum if text on image

### Card Images

- Consistent aspect ratio: 4:3 for service/blog cards, 1:1 for doctor portraits
- Object-fit: cover, centred on focal point (face for doctors)

### Gallery Images

- Mixed orientations acceptable; unified by consistent gap and border-radius
- Lightbox on click — no autoplay carousel

### WordPress-Sourced Images

- Use `altText` from CMS — never empty for informative images
- Fallback: branded placeholder with clinic logo watermark if image missing

---

## Spacing Philosophy

**Space is luxury.** The brand uses whitespace to communicate confidence and premium positioning.

- Sections breathe: minimum `py-16` mobile, `py-28` desktop
- Content never touches viewport edges: `px-4` minimum
- Related elements group tightly; unrelated elements separate generously
- When in doubt, add more space — not less
- Vertical rhythm: 8px base grid, 32px between related items, 64–96px between sections

---

## Medical Trust Guidelines

1. Display doctor full name, qualifications (MD, MBBS, specialisation), and professional photo on every doctor reference.
2. Link to regulatory body registrations where available.
3. Never display patient testimonials without attribution or consent.
4. Treatment pages must include "consultation required" messaging where outcomes vary.
5. Phone and address must be reachable within two taps on mobile.
6. SSL, privacy policy, and secure form submission must be evident.

---

## Luxury Brand Guidelines

1. **Restraint over excess** — one gold accent per section maximum
2. **Quality over quantity** — fewer sections, better executed
3. **Typography carries the design** — invest in font pairing and hierarchy
4. **No visual discounts** — no badges, stickers, starbursts, or promotional banners
5. **Consistency is premium** — identical card treatments, button styles, and spacing everywhere
6. **Silence is premium** — no autoplay, no pop-ups, no chat widgets that interrupt (future chat must be opt-in)

## Things the Brand Should NEVER Do

### Visual

- ❌ Stock photos of doctors with stethoscopes
- ❌ Bright red urgency banners or countdown timers
- ❌ Gradient backgrounds (except subtle image overlays)
- ❌ Drop shadows heavier than `shadow-sm`
- ❌ More than two font families
- ❌ Comic Sans, script fonts, or decorative display fonts in body text
- ❌ Auto-playing video or audio
- ❌ Carousel/slider for critical content
- ❌ Pop-ups, interstitials, or email capture overlays on first visit

### Verbal

- ❌ "Best", "cheapest", "only", "#1" without substantiation
- ❌ Fear-based marketing ("Don't let wrinkles ruin your life")
- ❌ Exclamation marks in body copy
- ❌ Medical claims without qualification
- ❌ Jargon without explanation

### Structural

- ❌ Hiding contact information behind multiple clicks
- ❌ Competing CTAs in the same viewport
- ❌ Generic template layouts that could belong to any clinic
- ❌ Dark patterns (hidden fees, pre-checked boxes, difficult unsubscribe)

### Technical

- ❌ Slow page loads that undermine premium positioning
- ❌ Broken images or missing alt text
- ❌ Layout shift during load
- ❌ Inaccessible forms or navigation
