# Healthcare UI Skill — Care Well Medical Centre

## When to Use

Apply when designing or implementing any user-facing interface for the medical centre website.

## Core Rules

- Design language: **Editorial Luxury Minimalism** for enterprise healthcare.
- Convey **trust, professionalism, and premium quality**.
- Never feel template-based — every section must feel custom-designed.
- Healthcare-appropriate tone: calm colors, clear typography, no aggressive marketing.

## Visual Standards

- **Colors**: Teal primary (#0D4F4F), gold accent (#C4A882), warm off-white sections.
- **Typography**: Serif headings (editorial) + sans-serif body (readable).
- **Spacing**: Generous whitespace — luxury feels spacious.
- **Imagery**: Client's existing WordPress photography; no generic stock medical images.
- **Cards**: Subtle borders, no heavy drop shadows.

## Trust Signals

- Doctor credentials and specializations prominently displayed.
- Certifications and years of experience visible.
- Clear contact information (phone, address) accessible from every page.
- Professional photography over stock imagery.

## Page Patterns

- **Hero**: Headline + subtitle + primary CTA + optional image.
- **Section**: Overline label + heading + description + content grid.
- **CTA**: Single clear action per section ("Book Consultation", "Contact Us").
- **Cards**: Image + title + excerpt + link for services, blogs, doctors.

## Do's

- Show credentials, certifications, and experience.
- Make phone number and contact CTAs prominent on mobile.
- Use high-quality photography from WordPress media library.
- Maintain calm, trustworthy visual tone throughout.

## Don'ts

- Do not use aggressive pop-ups, countdown timers, or dark patterns.
- Do not use bright saturated colors — healthcare demands calm trust.
- Do not use auto-playing video or audio.
- Do not use generic stock photos of doctors with stethoscopes.
- Do not use comic sans, script fonts, or decorative typefaces.
